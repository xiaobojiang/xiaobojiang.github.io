The relationship can be understood as follows:

1. SPU (Standard Product Unit): Represents a general product model (e.g., “Nike Air Max Sneakers”).

2. SKU (Stock Keeping Unit): Represents a specific variant of the product (e.g., “Nike Air Max Sneakers, Red, Size 10”).

3. EAN (European Article Number): Represents a globally unique identifier (barcode) for each specific SKU.

Relationship Breakdown:

• One SPU can have multiple SKUs (because a product can have different sizes, colors, or packaging).

• One SKU corresponds to one unique EAN (since each specific variation of the product has its own barcode).

• Therefore, one EAN = one SKU, which is a part of an SPU.

This structure ensures product grouping (SPU), inventory tracking (SKU), and universal identification (EAN).
