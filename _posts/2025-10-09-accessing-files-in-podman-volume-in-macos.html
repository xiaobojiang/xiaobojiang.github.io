Podman is a really nice software to run containers in Macos, and you can easily create volumes and used in container for persistence. Here are some tricks to inspects those files in your Mac OS file system. That could be useful to file operation with your familiar OS command.

Be careful on the difference where the Linux and Mac volume persistence location:

  • On Linux, Podman stores volumes under /var/lib/containers/storage/volumes/... on the host, so you can ls them directly.

  • On macOS, Podman runs inside a lightweight VM (called Podman Machine) managed by QEMU. The volume mountpoints live inside that VM, not on your macOS filesystem. That’s why $(podman volume inspect … --format '{{.Mountpoint}}') doesn’t resolve on the macOS side.

✅ Ways to inspect a Podman volume on macOS

1. Use a temporary container

This is the most straightforward:

podman run --rm -it -v mydata:/data alpine ls -al /data

You’ll see the files stored in mydata.

2. SSH into the Podman VM

podman machine ssh

Then inside the VM:

sudo ls -al /var/lib/containers/storage/volumes/mydata/_data

3. Copy data out of the volume

If you want to move files to your macOS host, just use your familiar cp command.

That will copy everything from the volume into your current working directory on macOS.
