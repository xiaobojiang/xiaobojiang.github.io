During the New Year holiday, I hopped on my Merida bike and went for a ride to Taicang, just outside Shanghai. The winter air was crisp, and the quiet roads made the journey especially enjoyable.

While in Liuhe Town, I made a stop at Tinglin Aozhao Restaurant and treated myself to a bowl of the classic Aozhao Noodles—a must-try local specialty.

Aozhao noodles have a long history, dating back to the Ming and Qing dynasties. They are known for their rich, dark soy-based broth, carefully simmered with pork bones, eel, and spices. Traditionally, the soup is served generously over chewy noodles and topped with ingredients like sliced pork, eel, and scallions. The name “Aozhao” comes from the traditional cooking method, which emphasizes slow braising to develop deep flavor.

It was a perfect combination of cycling, local culture, and comfort food—an unforgettable way to spend a New Year’s ride. 🚴‍♂️🍜
