Mistral has released a full life-cycle assessment of Mistral Large 2, tracking its environmental impact over 18 months. The analysis, conducted using the Frugal AI methodology developed by AFNOR (the French standards body), involved environmental consultancies and was peer-reviewed by independent auditors.

The study considered every stage of the chain: construction of data centers, manufacturing and transporting servers, training and inference, user equipment, and indirect impacts of usage.

Key results:

  • Greenhouse gases:Training produced 20,400 metric tons CO₂ — about the same as the annual emissions of 4,400 passenger cars.

  • Water use: 281,000 m³for cooling, equal to the consumption of a U.S. family of four over 500 years.

  • Stage breakdown:

    • Training & inference: 85.5% of GHG emissions, 91% of water use, 29% of material use.

    • Server manufacturing, transport & decommissioning: 11% of emissions,5% of water,61% of materials.

    • Network traffic: <1% across all measures.

Per interaction (400 tokens ~ one page of text):

  • 1.14 g CO₂, equivalent to watching a 10-second YouTube clip in the U.S. or 55 seconds in France.

  • 45 ml water (~3 tablespoons).

  • Material footprint similar to minting a €2 coin.

This is one of the most detailed, peer-reviewed looks so far at the full environmental cost of large language models.
